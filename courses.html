Open Water


This is the entry-level course for scuba diving. It is designed to equip you with the knowledge and skills necessary to dive safely and confidently in open water environments. During the course, you will learn about scuba diving equipment, basic diving skills, and dive planning. You will also complete several open water dives to practice your skills and gain experience. Advanced Open Water


This course builds on the skills and knowledge you learned in the Open Water Diver course. It is designed to expand your diving capabilities and introduce you to new types of diving. During the course, you will complete five adventure dives, including deep diving, navigation, and night diving. This course is a great way to gain experience and confidence in a variety of diving situations.

Rescue Diver


The Rescue Diver course is designed to teach you how to prevent and manage diving accidents. During the course, you will learn about dive first aid, emergency management, and rescue techniques. You will also practice rescuing other divers and responding to emergency situations. This course is an important step for anyone interested in becoming a professional diver.

Divemaster


The Divemaster course is the first level of professional diving. It is designed to teach you how to lead and manage diving activities. During the course, you will learn about dive theory, dive planning, and how to lead groups of divers. You will also complete practical exercises to develop your leadership and teaching skills. This course is a great way to take your diving to the next level and start a career in diving.
